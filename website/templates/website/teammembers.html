Dr. Yasin Mamatjan

    In the last 6 years, he has:
  • authored 34 journal publications, and over 82 conference posters/papers (16 as the first or co-first author), while I was the contributor for methylation analysis in 6 Nature papers.
  • developed ~20 ML models and genomics pipeline for clinical research laboratories.
  • trained three BSc students and obtained five grants in 2022 alone in the field of cancer classification and recommender systems for brain stroke risk and heart attack risk stratification.
  • contributed to equity, diversity, and inclusion by employing underrepresented students and engaging in high school outreach activities.
  • supported University Health Network (UHN) in maintaining and fostering a culture of research excellence and continue to collaborate with them.
  • developed predictor of meningioma recurrence used in the hospital for clinical test and published in Neuro-Oncology in 2019, which also led to a conference award and a “Moonshot Grant” from the US.
  • obtained research experiences at Philips Research Lab in Germany, Translational Genomics Laboratory (TGL) at OICR and Advanced Genomics Lab in UHN.
  • established collaborations with 20 research groups in five countries.
